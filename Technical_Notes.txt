A mixing console is just a bunch of channels of signal processors, and a summing amplifier.
The three main tools audio engineers need on a mixing console are:
Volume (slider/fader to turn things up and down)
Equalizer (controllable frequency/bandwidth filters + summing with the original signal)
Dynamic compression (automatically turn loud parts of audio down but leave quiet audio untouched)

Volume/pan - will implement with VCA, controlled by a motorized fader being read by a microcontroller.

EQ - will implement with OTAs, voltage controllable "resistance" (current scaling) - fully parametric, can use OTAs to control freq, bw, and gain, see the analog design folder: EQ for spice sims.

Compression - will implement with VCA, the control voltage envelope generated by the microcontroller possibly. (otherwise will need OTAs for some params)

Digital control - microcontroller (STM32G474), rotary encoders (EC11), faders/slide potentiometers read by a ADC, screens, LEDs to show position of knobs (shift registers), outboard DACs (on SPI bus) to generate the control voltages.
